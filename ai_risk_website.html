Key Findings & Conclusions
A summary of the most important insights from the analysis, modelling, and clustering stages.
🌲
Random Forest Outperforms
RF achieved 85.8% vs 49.9% for LR, showing the importance of non-linear modelling for survey data.
📊
4 Distinct Perception Groups
K-Means (k=4) found Low, Moderate, High, and Mixed risk awareness groups. Low Risk is largest at 36%.
🔍
Trust & Age Are Key Drivers
Trust in AI, year of birth, and ideology are the strongest predictors of risk group membership.
✅
Consistent Cross-Validation
RF CV accuracy was stable across all 5 folds (85.4%–86.1%), confirming the model generalises well.
🌍
Global Variation in Perception
Top countries show different AI concern distributions, reflecting cultural and political factors.
📈
Risk Score Skews Moderate
Most respondents score 1.0–2.0 on the AI Risk Score — moderate concern is the dominant sentiment.
